Sonos:
Mechanical Materials Database

When I started at Sonos, the team wanted to launch a materials project that organized and standardized a material-to-part-number association. The downstream effects of this would help Mechanical Engineers apply a material to their models, allow sales reports to acquire data on material sales for compliance audits, and allow for other potential uses. Much of this information is sensitive, so I will be using alternate images.



The Objective:
* Create an association between a material, including its mechanical properties, and the Sonos Part Number (SPN)
* Ensure that database is accessible company wide
* Compile as much information available

The Process:
I began by opening the CAD drawings in each of the BOMs. I listed the material name and part number of the model. I then researched the material properties on supplier websites, material’s data bases, and contacted the engineers in charge of the drawings if I couldn’t find the materials. I corroborated the information with multiple sources to ensure the most up-to-date data was being entered.

I compiled all the data that was readily available. After I finished an initial run-through of data collection for all the current Sonos products in mass production, I went though the list and carefuly searched for any missing information.

Challenges:
* Certain materials did not have all the properties I needed, so I acquired the best I could
* Very tedious work, but I managed my time and energy well to get it done
* Engineers in charge of certain drawings no longer worked at Sonos. This made it difficult to properly track a few materials
* Others were doing a similar job, but none were as extensive as mine, so I was able to help those that had an incomplete list


End Result:
* I presented my database to a broad audience which satisfied many who were seeking/attempting to track Sonos's material usage.
* This data allowed other teams to use in other areas outside of engineering(sales reports, environmental compliance, cost reduction, etc.).
* I developed a deep understanding of how materials are managed and how data flows through a company.
* I also enriched my knowledge in material science, specifically resins.
